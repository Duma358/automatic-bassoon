Clinical Decision Tool

A structured approach for patients with barriers to examination.

Start Here: Identify the Barrier

Which barrier best explains the patient's behaviour?

What if the barrier is unclear?

Perform a brief Context Scan (1–2 min): check vitals, analgesia timing, ward notes, and ask a ward ally for observations.

Consequence if skipped:

Risk of misattributing behaviour (e.g., agitation from hypoglycaemia or pain).

Outcome:

Either the barrier emerges (select a pathway above) or the patient stabilises with universal measures.

Pathway A: Sensory/Cognitive Barrier

Step 1: Preparation

YES: Gather insights, pre-stage equipment. Outcome: Smoother, faster exam.

NO: Approach unprepared. Consequence: Stop-start interaction, exam abandoned.

Practice: Announce self, narrate actions, ask consent, use spatial cues.

⬇
Step 2: Orientation & Narrated Exam

YES: Speak first, state role, describe each step. Outcome: Preserves dignity, yields essential data.

NO: Silent approach. Consequence: Fear, withdrawal, refusal.

⬇
Step 3: Check Treatable Contributors

YES: Screen for pain, hypoxia, glucose, meds. Outcome: Engagement improves without coercion.

NO: Persist without addressing physiology. Consequence: Escalation, inaccurate assessment.

Pathway B: Language/Cultural Barrier

Step 1: Qualified Language Support

YES: Use qualified interpreter. Outcome: Better comprehension, safer consent.

NO: Use ad-hoc interpreter. Consequence: Increased adverse events, inequity.

⬇
Step 2: Dual-Medium Communication

YES: Use home language for warmth, English for continuity. Outcome: Reduced mistrust.

NO: Use only one language. Consequence: Cold/transactional or low-retention.

⬇
Step 3: Values Alignment

YES: Limited self-disclosure, acknowledge beliefs respectfully. Outcome: Rapport without collusion.

NO: Over-share or debate beliefs. Consequence: Reinforce mistrust or trigger defensiveness.

Pathway C: Confrontational

Step 1: Validate First

YES: State "Your pain is real." Shift to functional goals. Outcome: Lowers arousal, opens collaboration.

NO: Lead with rebuttal. Consequence: Escalation, non-adherence.

⬇
Step 2: MI-Style Direction

YES: Use Motivational Interviewing (OARS). Outcome: Collaborative plan, safety maintained.

NO: Argue facts. Consequence: Power struggle, disengagement.

⬇
Step 3: Offer MDT & Non-Pharm Options

YES: Brief action plan (activity, sleep), psych/physio. Outcome: Aligns with guidance, fosters self-efficacy.

NO: Reassure-and-dismiss or chase opioids for CPP. Consequence: Dependency, persistent disability.

Universal Safety Net & References

Universal Safety Net

Summarise & Teach-back: Improves adherence, fewer bounce-backs.

MDT Framing: "We work as a team" reduces triangulation and stabilises the plan.

Escalation Pathway: Activate de-escalation protocol to control risk and salvage therapeutic relationship.

References & Rationale

Vision impairment: Introduce self, describe actions, consent before touch. (PMC, WHS, CMS)

Language access: Qualified interpreters lower error/malpractice risk. (Joint Commission, AHRQ, Journal of Ethics)

De-escalation: Project BETA consensus; concise, calm, collaborative. (PMC, PubMed, DBS Alliance)

Chronic pain: NICE NG193—holistic assessment, shared plans, favour non-pharm options. (NICE, PMC, ResearchGate)

This tool is intended for clinical decision support and does not replace professional medical judgment.
